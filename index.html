Landslide Susceptability

In June 2000, the Colorado Springs City Council approved a $6 million buyout of 27 homes in the city that had been destroyed by landslides. From then through 2016, there were 749 new residences built in natural areas. Almost all were built on or near landslide-susceptible zones.
